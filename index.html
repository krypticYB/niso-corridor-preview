Wall 01

Origins of Electricity in Nigeria

The story begins long before modern reform. Early generation, early utility structures, and early national coordination laid the foundation for the system that would eventually become a modern grid.

First recorded generation

Electricity generation began in Nigeria in 1896, marking the earliest stage of electrification and the start of a national infrastructure story that would continue across generations.

The first utility structure

The Nigerian Electricity Supply Company was established in 1929, representing the first formal utility milestone and an early attempt at organized power delivery.

National coordination takes shape

The Electricity Corporation of Nigeria emerged in 1950 and assumed broader responsibility in 1951, signaling a more coordinated framework for generation and distribution.

Wall 02

National Development

As national ambitions expanded, electricity moved from a limited service into a development instrument. Institutions evolved to serve a larger, more connected Nigeria.

ECN

Institutional build-out

The Electricity Corporation of Nigeria became a central point of coordination, helping shape an organized national supply structure.

1972

NEPA is formed

ECN and the Niger Dams Authority were merged to form the National Electric Power Authority, consolidating generation and power delivery under one institution.

Development era

From service provision to national utility identity

In this phase, electricity becomes inseparable from modernization, industrial growth, and public service delivery. The corridor wall presents this as a move from fragmented beginnings to national-scale ambition.

Wall 03

Reform & Unbundling

The power sector enters a reform era. Policy, law, and market restructuring reshape electricity from a centralized utility model into a multi-actor system.

National Electric Power Policy

The National Electric Power Policy launched a formal reform pathway and set the stage for deeper institutional restructuring.

EPSR Act and PHCN era

The Electric Power Sector Reform Act established the legal framework for a restructured electricity market, while PHCN became a transitional institutional vehicle.

Privatization and unbundling

Generation and distribution moved into privatized structures, while transmission remained government-owned. The sector framework became more visibly segmented into GENCOs, DISCOs, and TCN.

Wall 05

Emergence of NISO

The legislative and institutional pivot: a new mandate for system operation, market coordination, grid discipline, and reliability.

08 Jun 2023

Electricity Act assented

The Electricity Act 2023 signaled a decisive shift toward a more decentralized and multi-tiered power market.

Dec 2023

TCN split proposed

The federal government signaled plans to separate physical transmission assets from independent system operation.

2025

NISO inaugurated

NISO emerged as the independent system operator, taking charge of reliable system and market operations while TCN focuses on infrastructure.
